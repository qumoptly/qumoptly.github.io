DPSS EVOLUTION 15

This Q-switched Nd: YLF is intra cavity with a large 5X5X15 mm LBO crystal in a heater. The Nd: YLF rod is pumped by 4 CCP Coherent laser diode at 798 nm. Each diode produces 20W.The Q-switch is a 25W water cooled NEOS driven by an IntraAction QE-2750B This laser is optimized for pumping ultrafast Ti: Sapphire amplifying to obtain high power femtoseconds pulses; Specifications: 6 mJ at 1 kHz 3 mJ at 5 kHZ and 1.5 mJ at 10 kHz 250 nanosecond pulse width wavelength 527 nm diameter 3 mm multimode beam


This laser when I received it was a wreck, one of the cooling water lines had popped and the cooling water flowed into the aluminum case (corroded case bottom) .
The goal is to make it work again which is not a "thin case"

This laser is a DPSS q-switch using a Smith Smith cavity with a LBO doubler of 5x5x15 mm

The cavity contains a Nd: YLF l advantage of this crystal is that it produces polarized light which greatly improves frequency doubling
The other advantage is that it has a very low dn / dT coefficient, which means that it does not produce a thermal lens effect, so it is easy to maintain the fundamental mode even at high repetition rate. also a huge advantage for frequency doubling.
Third advantage not least the radiative life is 480-526 mico-seconds which allows to store a large impulse energy so still an enormous advantage for dubbing frequency!

The Nd: YLF reaches 1053 and 1047 nm, its absorption band is between 795 and 805 nm and it goes out in the green at 527 nm 12 mJ @ 1 kHz and 1.5 mJ @ 10 kHz.

This frequency doubled DPSS Q-switch has been developed by COHERENT for the pumping of regenerative amplifiers used in femtosecond Ti: Sapphire lasers!

It is able to generate pulses of 250 ns with an energy of 12 mJ is a peak power of 48 KW and 1000 pulses per second !!!!!!!!!

To obtain these performances the defining element is the Nd: YLF crystal which I already gave the characteristics to the previous post.

The crystal is pumped by 4 diodes of 20 W @ 798 nm. Each diode has its own separate power supply and its separate temperature management which makes it possible to control the diode emission frequency for optimum conversion efficiency. in the crystal. The crystal is cooled by water and found in a tube-shaped reflector with 4 slots 0.3 mm wide and 10 mm long to introduce the pumping radiation of the diodes.
This reflector is gilded inside and out.

A nice trick is the temperature regulation of the diodes which is not done by a Peltier TEC but as follows:

Each diode is mounted on a copper block which is traversed by the cooling water and on which is mounted a heating resistive element piloted by a Watlow Anafaze CLS 204. Ponc regulator we can work with tap water which normally is at 15 degrees Celsius and to maintain the optimum temperature of 20 degrees is the heating body that compensates.
The advantage of this system compared to Peltier is that it is more compact and more reactive.

The cavity is of the Fox Smith type, the frequency doubler is a 5x5x15mm LBO mounted in a thermostatic chamber regulated by a Watlow 96 PID.

The power supply is a NEWPORT 58 having 4 independent outputs. 40 amp pendant.
The Q-switch is a NEOS N36027-25-3-BR 25W water cooled and driver by an IntraAction QE 2750B generator. Actually each diode is controlled separately with its temperature sensor in the copper block (black cable covered with a brown sheath on 20mm there are 8 cables) and the 4 brown cables that arrive on them. small cartridges attached to the copper block are the 4

Heating elements. A temperature PID controller controls the temperature of the laser diodes to have an emission centered on the maximum absorption peak of the Nd: YLF.

You've seen the Q-switch at the Brewster Index trim faces to avoid reflection losses!
So the Nd: YLF bar must be oriented correctly with respect to the q-switch.
The most difficult thing in laser reshaping is the optical adjustment of the cavities because we do not have methods and procedures of manufacturer regulation. And with pulsed lasers it's more dangerous because you can create self-focusing and destroy the crystals!
