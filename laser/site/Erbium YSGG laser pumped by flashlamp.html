Erbium YSGG laser pumped by flashlamp



This laser consists of a 3mm x 60mm Er: Cr YSGG bar pumped by a 3 x 60mm flash tube into a cavity cooled by a forced circulation of de-ionized water allowing to work at a repetition rate of 20pps. The emission wavelength is 2.797 microns it is a 4-level laser. The output is on an optical fiber. The resonator consists of a 99.6% @ 2797nm totally reflective mirror and a 90% R mirror forming an unstable resonator r = -500mm to correct the lens effect of the amplifier bar

       


L e pumping is done with a flash tube for 300 microseconds with a pump energy of 80J / cm3 giving it to 3mm diameter rod 62mm x maximum energy of 30J. The duration of the transition 4 I 11/2 being shorter (1.2ms) than the level 4 I 13/2 (5.3ms) the pumping occurs by a complex process of energy transfer of the higher levels by green photons and blues emitted by the flash tube. It is therefore important to have the shortest flash duration (about 300 microseconds) to have an acceptable performance! The flash pumping efficiency is of the order of 1% and pumping by diode at 962 nm of the order of 7%.





The output energy in mJ as a function of the transmission of the output mirror is given for a YSGG bar pumped in QCW by a laser diode.
For a bar of the same size in Er, Cr TAG the output energy is 2 times smaller!
