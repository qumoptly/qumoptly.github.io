My second ruby laser   1969

This laser consists of a ruby 4 mm in diameter and 60 mm long. It is in the foyer of a polished aluminum chamber milled in an aluminum block. At the other focus is a linear flash tube with a diameter of 6 mm inside and 60 mm long.
The threshold to obtain the laser effect is 190 J in 1 ms.
The maximum stored energy in the energy state E2 = 2.18 J / cm3. At the level of the flash lamp the energy to obtain the laser threshold is between 100-300 J / cm3 depending on the concentration of the Cr+3 ions of the diameter of the bar of its length and the reflection power of the mirrors.
The electrical power of the xenon lamp must be 70kW/cm3
The Brewster angle for a ruby is 30° 34'
The gain signal for a passage in the ruby Go = 1.1 for a neodymium glass Go = 2.3 and for a YAG Go = 1720 Conclusion A YAG can not store energy in the E3 state which limits the energy to 300mJ for a bar of 5mm diameter triggered mode and 900mJ for a 9mm diameter bar.		 The ruby laser is a laser with 3 levels: during the discharge in the flash tube the photons emitted by it are directed by the elliptical reflector on the ruby bar doped with 0.05% chromium and therefore the electrons of the E1 ground state are pumped on the energy state E3 by absorption of photons corresponding to this energy difference.
To reach the minimum threshold of population inversion that makes it possible to obtain the laser effect in a 3-level system, the atomic domain must be in a state of excitation E3. This involves a lot of pump energy to reach the laser threshold, which is not the case with a 4-level system like YAG: Nd
