YAG laser tripped CONTINUUM from QUANTEL



The QUANTEL laser consists of an oscillator triggered by a Pockels cell. The resonator is of the "Unstable" type with a Gaussian-type convex exit mirror which makes it possible to obtain a monomode beam of large diameter and diffraction limit before being injected into the amplifier module.
The YAG bar has a diameter of 6 mm and 100mm long with the faces cut at a 2-degree angle to avoid amplification by fluorescence, on each side of the bar are blades quarter d wave, then a polarizer and a Pockels cell followed by a totally reflective concave mirror.
I also have an extra-frequency frequency doubler that allows Megawatts pulses at 532nm diffraction limit.
