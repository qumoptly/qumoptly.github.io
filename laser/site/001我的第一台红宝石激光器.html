001 My first ruby laser 1968



This is the first laser I built at the age of 18 in 1968 in Geneva





  Ruby laser pumped by a helical flash tube of 7 kJ output 22 J in 2 ms!
 

I built this laser in 1968 with which I participated in the competition "Science Calls the Young" and obtained a 2nd prize in physics with a study trip to England.
The flash tube is Glass & Quartz manufacturing PARIS, I worked 1 month at the post office in Geneva during the summer holidays in 1967 to pay me this tube.
The ruby ​​was graciously polished by Hrand Djevahirdjan de Monthey in Switzerland.
The capacitors HYDRAWERK 47 uF / 6 kV, I bought them at the recovery of CERN for 20 FrS / piece,I buy 12 units!

 


                                                                                                                                                                                                              

              
               
Here is a calculator to optimize the capacity and the self of the LC circuit to the characteristics of the flash tube.
For this tube a capacity of 125 uF and a self of 1300 uH gives an energy of 2500 J to 6300V of load being at 8.8% of the explosion energy of the tube!

  
         



Currently I have started manufacturing a power supply for the flash tube.
It consists of a capacitor bank of 8 x 125 uF / 3kV.
We can choose between 1 and 8 capacities in parallel.
I use a self I had reeled in 1967 at my grandfather (retired engineer SECHERON Geneva) this self consists of 4 chokes that can be connected in series or in parallel.
I will use a system "serie triggering" which simplifies the assembly and moreover I have a transformer series.
In the same cart I also have an electrolytic capacitor bank of 10kJ under 1350V.
Electrolytic capacitors are well suited to ruby laser pumping because of their high internal inductance and internal resistance. They are not suitable for YAG pumping.
