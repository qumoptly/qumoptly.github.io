COHERENT Speed ​​femtosecond laser



Coherent's SPEED laser is a sub-100 femtosecond oscillator using a sapphire titanium crystal pumped by a continuous 5-6W laser at 532nm.
It consists of an INVAR board on which all the optical components are fixed. The crystal Ti: S acting from medium to gain (amplifier).
Four NDM mirrors that are negative dispersion mirrors to compensate for the enlargement of the femtosecond pulse propagating in the laser cavity.
This cavity consists of a totally reflective mirror mounted on an electromagnetic coil provided with a spring to change the length of the cavity quickly which triggers the passage of the continuous regime. in femtosecond regime.
A diaphragm favors the central modes which makes it possible to obtain the blocking of the modes in the Ti: S crystal by the KERR effect (nonlinear change of the refractive index).
At the other end of the cavity is the semi-transparent exit mirror. A lens is used to focus the pump beam of a 6W DPSS laser doubled in frequency in the Ti: S crystal.
Ti: S is mounted on a TEC to dissipate heat and keep the crystal at a constant temperature.
The repetition frequency is 80MHz directly from the length of the cavity.
This laser is very compact this by the fact of using 4 mirrors NDM (patent COHERENT)
The diameter of the femtosecond beam is 1.2mm with a divergence of 0.75mrad M M2 = 1.1 horizontally polarized 800nm ​​line width 12nm
