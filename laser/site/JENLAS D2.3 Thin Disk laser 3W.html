JENLAS D2.3 Thin Disk laser 3W



The JENLAS D2.3 laser is a YAG-doped thin-film laser doped with ytterbium. The pumping is done in 4 passes of a pump beam emitted by a laser diode at 940nm which can work continuously or pulsed regime. The light emitted by the diode 15 is focused by means of the lenses 16 and 17 on the thin disc (0.1mm) and returned to the concave mirror 18 which the referral on the thin disc provided with a mirror HR for 940 and 1030nm. The IR beam is focused on the doubler crystal LBO by the output mirror 9.

The great feature of this laser is that it is very compact for the continuous power at 532nm that it delivers with the thin disk architecture. It will replace my argon laser which consumes nearly 10 kW for 5W of output that will allow me to pump the laser MIRA900 femtosecond.

 

The JENLAS D2.3 laser requires a laser diode power supply of 2V maximum 25A to operate. A 2 x 6V DC power supply for the 2 TECs to cool the pump diode. A power supply for the internal TEC used to maintain the temperature of the LBO crystal doubler as well as the 2 temperature control system.

The 532nm laser output is horizontally polarized 100: 1 with a maximum power of 5W TEM00. The diameter of the laser beam is 2mm.
