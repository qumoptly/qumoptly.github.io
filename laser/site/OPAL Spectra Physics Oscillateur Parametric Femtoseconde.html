OPAL Spectra Physics Oscillator Parametric Femtosecond

Optical parametric oscillators (OPOs) were used for the first time in the mid-1960s, an alternative to dye lasers to broaden the frequency spectrum especially in the infrared.

Opale is the first pulsed-mode femtosecond parametric oscillator laser (SPPO)
with the following features:
• Ti: pumped sapphire
• Adjusted temperature
• Fully automatic cavity length adjustment with active stabilization
• Automated scanning and configuration of lengths of d wave
• Output power> 150 mW at 1.3 μm and 1.5 μm
• Output pulse width <130 fs
• Synchronized signal and Idler outputs with wavelength coverage of 1.1 to 2.6 μm.

Opal is entirely a solid laser, it does not use any laser dye or a cryogenic crystal. It uses a lithium triborate (LBO) pumped by a femtosecond laser.

Nonlinear crystal LBO (Lithium Triborate) with 2 sides cut at the Brewster angle mounted on a thermoelectric heating disc with a temperature probe for regulating it. The pumping can be done between 720 and 850nm with an average power of 2W and pulses of 100fs.
The phase agreement for this crystal is non-critical and the temperature makes it possible to scan the spectrum between 1.1 and 1.6 micron and the idler between 1.6 and 2.4 micron.
