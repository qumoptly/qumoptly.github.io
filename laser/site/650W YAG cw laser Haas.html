650W YAG cw laser Haas



This laser head is intended to be used to make a laser doubled at 532nm output 300W. I will still need a 20kW power supply to run the 2 krypton tubes.


here I have just started the construction of a YAG pumped by 2 Krypton tubes of 10 kW each or 20 kW pump on a bar Nd: YAG 170 mm x 8 mm out already more than 650W cw I opted for a mounting Z for reasons of limitation of the diffraction effect of the YAG bar as it is pumped by krypton tubes!
I'm going to use a 5x5x15 mm BBO with Brewster windows mounted on a TEC with PID control for doubled frequency at 532 nm with 350 to 420W output!  
The BBO is a birefrengent negative uniaxial crystal that I will use in type I phase agreement. The BBO has 2 advantages and 1 disadvantage! Advantage Indeed, if the optical indices vary with the wavelength, they also vary with the temperatures!
As the power is very high more than 400W expected it is necessary to find a crystal whose optical index is very insensitive to temperature which is the case of BBO d (ne) / dT = -9.3.10e-6 1 / K and d (n0) / dT = - 16.6.10e-6 1 / K
The second advantage is that its resistance to the pump flow is 20 times higher than a KTP (several GW / cm2)
The disadvantage is it is slightly hygroscopic which necessitates closing it in an airtight cavity under an oxygen atmosphere!
The optimum working temperature will be 15 degrees Celsius: my BBO I bought it at CLEVELAND USA.
It is cut at 90 degrees to the optical axis.
