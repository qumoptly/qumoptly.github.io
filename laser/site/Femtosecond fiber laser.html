FIBER FEMTOSECOND LASER AND PASSIVE MODE LOCKING WITH A SESAM MIRROR.

This femtosecond passive mode locking laser model CALMAR LASERS FPL-M2UFFLUM22 , uses a SESAM saturable absorption semiconductor (mirror 125) and as an amplifying medium a Ytterbium doped fiber (130) which is pumped by a laser diode. 970nm (150)
One of the mirror of the resonator is composed of a holographic diffraction grating (112) and it makes it possible to change the output frequency of the laser pulses between 1030 and 1065 nm.
This module is an architecture insensitive to vibrations and shocks and it presents an exceptional stability and a maximum reliability and this without any adjustment!
The pulse duration can be specified during manufacture between 500fs and 5ps depending on the architecture of the fiber (length thereof).
This laser will allow us to perform the experiment of pulses measurement by multi-shot interferometric optical autocorrelation , using a GaP photodiode in 2-photon absorption regime. http://www.swissrocketman.fr/realisation-autocorelateur,fr,8,127.cfm
