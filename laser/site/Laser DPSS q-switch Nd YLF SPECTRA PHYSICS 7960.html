DPSS q-switch laser Nd: YLF SPECTRA PHYSICS 7960



This DPSS laser consists of a laser head, a 27.12MHz HF generator to excite the acousto-optical modulator for working in triggered mode, a power supply for the laser diode and the TEC.
The laser head comprises a 5mm diameter Nd: YLF crystal pumped into a hemi-spherical cavity on one of the faces of the bar by a fiber laser diode at 810nm.
An acousto-optic Q-switch is placed between the Nd: YLF bar and the output mirror.
The pump inlet face of the crystal and the output mirror form the laser cavity. The pump light is coupled in the bar by a lens which couples the pump beam with the volume of the TEM00 fundamental mode.
The q-switch is the element that has a variable loss in the cavity. An RF generator can control the laser pulses between 1Hz and 50kHz. Unique pulses can be generated.
The q-switch consists of a block of optical quality glass on which are glued transducers producing acoustic waves in the glass. These acoustic waves produce refractive index variations that act as a diffraction grating and produce a large loss in the cavity. When the q-switch is excited there is no laser effect. When the excitation of the q-switch cell is cut off, a laser oscillation takes place in the cavity that comes out in the form of a giant impulse!
