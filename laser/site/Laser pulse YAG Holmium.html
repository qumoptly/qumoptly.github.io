YAG pulsed laser: Holmium



 
The YAG: Cr: Tm: Ho laser is a 4-level laser emitting at 2100nm pulses of 0.2-3.5J with a repetition rate of 5Hz-50Hz. This VERSAPULSE medical laser was used to destroy kidney stones because it creates acoustic shock waves in liquid media.


The Cr ion acts as a broadband bandwidth of the flashlamp continuum radiation and efficiently transfers energy to the Tm ions.
A cross-relaxation process results in two Tm ions excited in the upper laser level 3H for every absorbed pump photon.
The Ho ion is directly excited in the upper level by non-radiative transfer from the 3H, Tm level. The laser transitions for Tm and Ho terminate in the highest levels of multiple ground state, thus both are "near-3-level" lasers.
